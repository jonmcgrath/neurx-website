Residual clot is not a cosmetic failure. It is an independent mortality predictor.

When adherent, organized thrombus is left behind after aspiration thrombectomy, the clinical consequences are severe and well-documented. Partially fibrinolyzed, cross-linked clot has fundamentally different physical properties than fresh thrombus. Vacuum aspiration cannot generate sufficient interfacial shear stress to dislodge it from the vessel endothelium — and this is a first-principles physical constraint, not an engineering gap.

Physicians accept incomplete removal because no device has been engineered specifically to solve this problem. Until now.

  • 4× Higher PE-related mortality in patients with residual thrombus burden after thrombectomy
  • 16× Increased right heart failure at 30 days versus patients who achieve complete clot removal
  • 10× Higher rate of VTE recurrence at one year in patients with significant residual thrombus

The –29 inHg Aspiration Ceiling

Every commercially available thrombectomy system — Inari FlowTriever, Inari ClotTriever, Penumbra Lightning Flash, Imperative Symphony — operates on vacuum aspiration at a maximum of approximately –29 inHg. This is sufficient for soft, fresh, mobile thrombus. It is mechanically insufficient against organized, adherent clot that has undergone partial fibrinolysis and endothelial attachment. No amount of pump engineering overcomes this constraint. It is a physical law, not a design choice.

How the NeuRx Sweeper works.

NeuRx Sweeper · Iliac Vein · Simulated Deployment
01
Advance beyond the thrombus
With the capture node in its collapsed 6-French profile, the NeuRx Sweeper is advanced over a standard 0.035" guidewire to a position distal to the target thrombus — past the clot, not through it. This distal approach is critical: the capture mechanism engages from the far side of the adherent mass.
02
Deploy the nitinol capture node
A single proximal handle retraction releases the superelastic nitinol capture node, which expands radially into a conical capture geometry. The node self-sizes to circumferentially oppose the vessel wall — providing uniform contact across the full luminal diameter. A locking mechanism maintains deployment during the sweep.
03
Retract — applying axial traction force
The operator retracts the deployed catheter proximally. The expanded capture node contacts the thrombus-endothelium interface and applies direct axial mechanical traction — physically dislodging adherent organized clot from the vessel wall and sweeping it proximally. This force is not subject to the –29 inHg ceiling. It is a direct mechanical contact interaction, not fluid dynamics.
04
Clot delivered to aspiration
The dislodged thrombus mass is concentrated and advanced into the aspiration lumen of the co-deployed catheter, where it is removed by conventional vacuum aspiration. The NeuRx Sweeper solves the physics problem that prevents aspiration from engaging adherent clot — then lets the aspiration system do what it does best.
05
Collapse, reposition, repeat if needed
The capture node is collapsed by advancing the inner shaft proximally. The device can be repositioned for additional passes targeting adjacent segments. Multiple passes are performed within the same procedure under existing CPT codes 37187 and 37188 — no additional billing codes, no new capital equipment, no workflow disruption.
